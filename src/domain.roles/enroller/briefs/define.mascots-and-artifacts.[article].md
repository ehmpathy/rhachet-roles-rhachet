# define: mascots and artifacts

## .what

role registries use two layers of symbolism:

```
🐙 mascot = animal that represents the whole registry
🎭 artifact = item that represents a specific role
```

- **mascots** = animals that embody the registry's philosophy
- **artifacts** = items/objects wielded by specific roles

## .why

mascots and artifacts create memorable, cohesive identity:

- **mascots** anchor the registry's philosophy in a vivid metaphor
- **artifacts** make roles tangible and distinct within the registry
- together they form a visual language for the ecosystem

## .the distinction

| layer | scope | examples |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **mascot** | whole registry | 🐢 sea turtle, 🦉 owl, 🐙 octopus |
| **artifact** | specific role | 🐚 shell, 📚 books, 🎭 mask |

mascots are always animals — they represent the whole.
artifacts are always items — they represent roles.

## .pattern

when you declare mascots in a readme:

```md
# mascots

this repo houses roles for [mascots] [emoji] — [philosophy phrase].

they wield:
- [artifact emoji] [artifact] — for [role]s — [purpose phrase]
```

## .guidelines

### mascot selection

the mascot should:
- be an animal (not an object, not a person)
- embody the registry's core philosophy
- have traits that map to the registry's principles

### artifact selection

each artifact should:
- be an item/object (not an animal, not a person)
- relate naturally to the mascot's world
- represent what the role "wields" to do its work
- have a clear purpose phrase

### consistency within a registry

- all roles in a registry share the same mascot
- each role has a unique artifact
- artifacts should feel cohesive (same aesthetic universe)
